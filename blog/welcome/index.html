Changing the way we work with time series

January 28, 2020

Mike Kuklik

Founder & CEO

The way we work with business/economic/financial time-series hasn’t changed much for almost two decades. It is high time to rethink the way we work with time series and bring the best time-series visualization, analytics, collaboration, and storage solutions to the modern enterprise and scientific community.

Microsoft Excel is still a dominant tool for visualization, analysis, and storage of time series. But as a general purpose tool, it is not efficient and convenient for working with time series. For example, changing data frequency, say daily to weekly, is tricky but often necessary for data comparison and visualization. Moving averages, smoothing, and detrending is not straight forward either.

The most common way to collaborate and share time-series data is by sending spreadsheets via a group email. But data is often changing due to errors and restatements. This results in multiple emails with different versions of the spreadsheet ending in “final1”, “final2”, etc. It is a spreadsheet nightmare and a major cause of errors and deterioration in data quality. This data is often used for decision making (pricing, investment, marketing spending, etc), which can have significant financial consequences.

Python and R are great tools for analysis of large time-series datasets and automation of mundane tasks, it is quite cumbersome for quick exploration, analysis, and visualization; that is why so many advanced Python and R users that I know, move data to Excel whenever they need to analyze and visualize data quickly. Furthermore, the steep learning curve makes them inaccessible to a wide group of business analysts who work with time series used for decision making by the managers.

There is a lack of a solution for the storage and management of time series, which is flexible and accessible to all users regardless of their technical proficiency. Today, analyzing, exploring, and visualizing time series is hard and cumbersome.

At ChronosDB we want to reinvent the way people work with time series. We are proud to introduce the first time-series database for business, economic, and financial time series. It will be a foundation for developing tools for time series intelligence, visualization, econometric analytics, and data quality management. This is only the beginning of our journey to achieve this vision.

ChronosDB is available as a SaaS solution at tshub.io, the time series hub.
